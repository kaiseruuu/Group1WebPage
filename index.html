Science

The term "science" comes from the Latin word "scientia," which means knowledge founded on demonstrable and reproducible data. Science is the systematic pursuit and application of knowledge and understanding of the natural and social worlds. Science is divided into three branches: physical science (physics and chemistry), life science (biology, ecology, and environmental science), and Earth Science (geology, meteorology, astronomy, and oceanography).

Physical Science

Physical science is the study of various steps related to the inorganic world, as opposed to organic world study, which is the main branch of biological science. Physics, chemistry, earth science, geology, space science, astronomy, materials science, and other disciplines are covered. In contrast to life science, physical science is an important component of natural science that deals with the study of non-living processes/systems.

Life Science

Life science is one of the branches of science that study living things. The life sciences are scientific fields that study living organisms such as plants, animals, and humans. Life sciences investigate the biology of how these organisms live, which is why this group of specialties is sometimes referred to as biology.

Earth Science

The term "earth science" is commonly used to refer to the study of the earth's atmosphere (meteorology or atmospheric science), the water flowing on and beneath the surface of continents (hydrology), and the earth's seas and oceans (oceanography or ocean sciences). Earth science is the study of the Earth's structure, properties, processes, and the planets that orbit it. This branch of science studies the Earth's structure, processes, and properties.
